Design Document v6 — Space Miner Idle (click to expand)

Game Overview

Space Miner Idle is a Roblox idle/simulator game where players mine asteroids in a 3D asteroid field, collect ore, refine it into bars, and spend those bars on upgrades. The game features 7 ore tiers arranged in concentric rings around the player's space port, with increasing difficulty, danger, and reward as players move to outer rings.

Target audience: Roblox players ages 8-16 who enjoy idle/simulator games (Bee Swarm Simulator, Mining Simulator, Pet Simulator style).

Session target: 30-60 minute initial session, with meaningful progression checkpoints every 5-10 minutes.

Core Loop

Mine Asteroid → Collect Ore → Refine into Bar → Spend Bars on Upgrades → Repeat

  • Mine: Click/hit asteroids to break them. Each has an HP value based on tier. Your pick determines damage per hit and which tiers you can mine.
  • Collect: Destroyed asteroids drop ore. Walk over it (or click in prototype) to pick up. Limited by carry capacity.
  • Refine: Return to your space port and deposit ore into refiners. Each refiner processes one ore at a time with a visible progress bar.
  • Spend: Completed bars are added to your inventory. Spend them on upgrades across 4 categories: Mining, Buildings, Combat, and Cosmetics.

Combat & Danger System

Tiers 4-7 have hostile enemies near the asteroids. In the Roblox version, these will be real-time enemies; in this prototype, combat is abstracted as a danger chance per mining hit.

  • Each hit on a Tier 4+ asteroid rolls against the danger chance. If triggered, the player takes damage.
  • Shield absorbs damage first, then HP. Shield regenerates after a delay when not taking damage.
  • Death (HP ≤ 0): Player loses all carried ore and is teleported back to the space port. HP resets to full.
  • Combat upgrades (Weapon Damage, Fire Rate) reduce the effective danger chance, making outer rings safer over time.
  • The Armory building must be purchased before any combat upgrades become available.

Multi-Bar Cost System

This is a key design pillar. Upgrades cost bars (not ore), and costs escalate from single-bar to multi-bar as the player progresses:

Progression PhaseCost PatternExample
Early (Tier 1-2)Single bar type10 Iron
Mid (Tier 3-4)Two bar types8 Emeralite + 10 Cobalt
Late (Tier 5-7)Three bar types8 Voidstone + 8 Aurium + 12 Emeralite

Why this matters: Multi-bar costs keep all tiers of mining relevant throughout the game. A late-game player still needs Iron and Copper for certain upgrades, preventing lower tiers from becoming useless. It also creates interesting resource allocation decisions — do you mine Tier 3 for Cobalt or Tier 5 for Aurium when both are needed?

Design Principles

  • No wasted tiers: Multi-bar costs ensure every ore type stays relevant. Players never "outgrow" a ring entirely.
  • Clear gates: Pick level is the hard gate. You literally cannot damage higher-tier asteroids without the right pick.
  • Risk/reward: Outer rings are more efficient per bar but dangerous. Players choose safety vs. speed.
  • Idle-friendly: Drones + auto-collector mean AFK players still progress, but active players progress faster.
  • Visible progression: Cosmetics + station upgrades give visible markers of progress to other players (important for Roblox social dynamics).
  • Session flexibility: Save system + offline progress means players can leave and return without losing momentum.

Roblox Implementation Notes

This prototype abstracts several systems that will be fully realized in Roblox Studio:

  • Movement: 3D flight with jetpack physics, not click-to-mine. Speed/thruster upgrades will feel tangible.
  • Combat: Real-time enemies patrolling outer rings. Weapon/shield upgrades affect actual combat mechanics.
  • Multiplayer: Each player has their own space port on a shared asteroid field. Players can see each other's cosmetic upgrades.
  • Cosmetics: Full 3D models placed in/on the space port, visible to all players.
  • Sound/VFX: Mining impacts, refiner hums, danger alerts, ambient space audio.
